New in v1.13.0

The board got out of your way.

This release sharpens the daily loop — a board that shows only live work, power-user search, and a tighter ticket cockpit.

Board

Closed falls off the board ✨

“Closed” is no longer a column. Resolve a ticket and the card plays a fall-off animation as it drops off the board; closed work is hidden by default and one toggle away when you need it. The Kanban fills the page width — no horizontal scrolling.

Search

Advanced search, now with regex

Match tickets with a case-insensitive POSIX regular expression across title, summary, description, company, number, and priority — alongside the status, company, assignee, and label facets. Invalid patterns are caught before they run.

Cockpit

A denser ticket cockpit

The ticket modal was tightened to stop wasting space: status and priority pair on one row and every field uses a consistent floating-label control, so the whole record reads at a glance.

Mail

Resilient email-to-ticket

IMAP ingest is idempotent on Message-ID, so an email delivered to two mailboxes — or replayed on a re-poll — can’t spawn duplicate tickets or wedge a mailbox. Connection errors now name the real server reason.

Why AnchorDesk

A helpdesk and an operations console, in one place.

Start with local tickets and add mail, identity, network discovery, PSA, and RMM integrations as you need them.

Local-first ticketing

Create, assign, filter, and update tickets in card or Kanban views. The board fills the page and stays focused on live work — closed tickets fall away. Configurable public ticket numbers stay independent of internal database IDs.

Ticket cockpit

Work the ticket from one view: details, company & contact, assignee, notes, time tracking, linked devices, script jobs, and email.

Device inventory & network map

LAN probes push discovered devices into your database. A Network view renders them as a live radial map — online/offline, open ports — and links them to tickets.

Act on machines

See live Tactical agent state when a ticket opens, then run scripts immediately or on a schedule and retain their output against the ticket.

SSO, MFA & RBAC

Local accounts, OIDC (Azure AD, Authentik, Okta…), and SAML 2.0 side by side — with TOTP MFA on by default and admin/technician/readonly roles enforced everywhere.

Multi-identity email

Poll multiple IMAP mailboxes, tag outbound subjects with [#NNNNN] for resilient threading, and reply as a shared box or personal alias with Cc/Bcc, signatures, templates, and inline images.

Live updates & SLA

A WebSocket channel keeps lists, the board, and open tickets current in real time, with a notification bell. Per-priority/company SLA timers show live countdown chips and raise at-risk and breach alerts.

Attachments, export & fuzzy search

Drag-drop files (local disk or any S3-compatible store like MinIO/R2/B2), download a printable ticket with inline activity, and find anything with typo-tolerant Postgres search across the whole conversation.

Admin console

See operational counts and recent activity, then manage users, auth, integrations, sync providers, mailboxes, probes, devices, and audit events.

Audit history & MCP

Browse actor-attributed changes across managed records, while authenticated MCP clients — minted with per-user personal access tokens — work with tickets, notes, time, and history as a real user.

My Day time view

A per-tech day-spread of logged time on a vertical clock — overlap-aware lanes, a live "now" line, and labelled gap bands that make the holes in a day obvious. Click any block to open the ticket.

How it works

Your database is the core. Everything else is an adapter.

  1. 1

    Tickets land locally

    Create them in the UI, ingest them from IMAP, or pull them from ConnectWise — the working record lives in PostgreSQL.

  2. 2

    Probes report devices

    A scanner on the customer LAN heartbeats and pushes device records, upserted into your devices table.

  3. 3

    Link & act

    Attach devices to a ticket and run RMM scripts against them — run-now or scheduled — without leaving the ticket.

  4. 4

    Work stays attributable

    Managed-record changes append actor and before/after data for ticket history and the Admin audit viewer.
